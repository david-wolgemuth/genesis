#+TITLE: Genesis Concept Tree
#+STARTUP: overview

* Prime
Cycle 2: Completed the spec alignment. Replaced render/ (SVG/HTML from Rust) with
serialize/ (JSON frame data). Added Three.js viewer. Ran 3 seeds: Tidal Pool (1213
bonds, 53.7% bonded), Deep Ocean (307 bonds, 17.5% bonded), Volcanic Ridge (1088
bonds, 36.1% bonded). Conservation held on all runs. Zero bonds ever broke — stable
chemistry regime. Priority next: Arrhenius kinetics to create temperature-dependent
bond rates and enable actual bond breaking in hot zones.

* World                                                        :branch:
** Grid and Terrain                                            :implemented:
   2D grid with elevation/depth per cell. Temperature and pressure
   derived from depth. Loaded from seed config.
** Star Energy                                                 :implemented:
   Cosine sweep for day/night cycle. UV attenuation underwater.
   Energy input per cell varies with angle to star and depth.
** Diffusion                                                   :implemented:
   Free agents drift toward lower-concentration neighbors.
   Fick's first law, discretized on the grid.
** Clocks                                                      :implemented:
   Three-tier tick hierarchy: stellar (1/cycle), world (~10/cycle),
   agent (~1000/cycle). Inner loop skips cold cells.
** Pressure and Depth                                          :shallow:
   Pressure is linear with depth. Could add nonlinear effects,
   compressibility, or pressure-driven currents later.
** Geothermal Vents                                            :implemented:
   Point sources of energy on the grid floor. Configurable count
   and output. Placement from seed config.
** Tidal Mechanics                                             :discovered:
   Periodic water level changes driven by orbital mechanics.
   Would create mixing zones and periodic exposure/submersion.
** Erosion and Sedimentation                                   :discovered:
   Slow terrain evolution. Requires long timescales and
   material transport model.
** Atmospheric Chemistry                                       :discovered:
   Free elements in the atmosphere reacting. Greenhouse effects,
   UV shielding. Requires gas-phase reaction model.
** Temperature Dynamics                                        :discovered:
   Currently temperature resets to depth-derived baseline every world
   tick. Exothermic bonds should locally heat cells. Endothermic bonds
   should locally cool them. This feedback would enable bond-breaking
   in hot zones (geothermal runaway).

* Agents                                                       :branch:
** Entity-Component Model                                      :implemented:
   Agents are bags of components. No type hierarchy. An element
   is an agent. A composite is an agent. Same architecture.
** Bonding                                                     :implemented:
   Adjacent agents evaluate bond rules from elements.toml.
   Exothermic bonds form spontaneously if shape-compatible.
   Endothermic bonds require energy input from cell.
   Bond stability depends on temperature and pressure.
** Bond Breaking                                               :implemented:
   Bonds break when local conditions exceed stability limits.
   In practice no bonds broke in runs 0002-0004 — temperatures
   stay below stability thresholds. See Temperature Dynamics.
** Neighborhood Interaction                                    :implemented:
   Agents interact only with adjacent cells. No action at distance.
** Catalysis                                                   :shallow:
   Catalyst presence multiplies bond formation rate. Currently
   simple multiplier. Could model enzyme kinetics (Michaelis-Menten).
** Reaction Kinetics (Arrhenius)                               :discovered:
   k = A * exp(-Ea / (R * T)). Rate constant as function of
   temperature. Would make bonding temperature-sensitive. HIGH PRIORITY
   next cycle — would create real spatial differentiation and enable
   bond-breaking in geothermal zones.
** Metabolism                                                   :discovered:
   Agents that consume elements/energy and produce others.
   Requires internal state and energy accounting.
** Replication                                                  :discovered:
   Template-based self-copying with imperfect fidelity.
   Variation is mandatory. Requires internal structure model.
** Membranes                                                    :discovered:
   Boundary components that contain other agents. Selective
   permeability. Requires osmotic pressure (Van't Hoff).
** Motility                                                     :discovered:
   Self-directed movement consuming energy. Requires
   energy budget and directional decision-making.

* Serialize                                                    :branch:
** Snapshot System                                             :implemented:
   Captures full grid state as JSON frames at configurable intervals.
   Per-cell: elevation, temperature, pressure, energy, agent_count,
   bonded_count, max_complexity, dominant_element, activity.
** Event Log                                                   :implemented:
   JSON log of simulation events. Timestamped, typed.
** Curator                                                     :implemented:
   Analyzes event log and produces camera.json with keyframes for
   first bond, first catalysis, bond milestones, and overview shots.
** Narrator                                                    :implemented:
   Reads event log and writes narrator.md. Plain English field-
   researcher log: births, trajectories, final state, recommendations.
** Frame Manifest                                              :implemented:
   manifest.json per run: run name, seed, element colors, frame
   file list. Entry point for the viewer.
** Run Comparison                                              :shallow:
   The curator currently does not compare against prior runs.
   Adding cross-run diffing would let the curator flag regressions
   and unexpected changes.

* Viewer                                                       :implemented:
   Three.js viewer (viewer/viewer.html). Loads manifest.json and
   frame data. Renders terrain as 3D heightmap with vertex colors
   blended from element type and activity intensity. Orbit controls,
   play/pause/scrub, speed control, guided camera (follows camera.json
   keyframes with annotation overlays), G key to toggle free orbit.
   Written once, works for all runs via ?run=run-NNNN query param.

* Conservation                                                 :implemented:
  Element counts verified after every world tick. Panic on violation.
  The single most important invariant. Held on all runs 0001-0004.

* Science                                                      :branch:
** Thermodynamics                                              :shallow:
   Gibbs free energy for bond evaluation. Currently simplified
   to energy_released/required thresholds. Could add entropy term.
** Fick's Laws (Diffusion)                                     :implemented:
   First law discretized on grid. Flux proportional to
   concentration gradient. Diffusion coefficient from element mass.
** Arrhenius Equation                                          :discovered:
   k = A * exp(-Ea / RT). Rate constant as function of
   temperature. Would make bonding temperature-sensitive. PRIORITY.
** Van't Hoff Equation                                         :discovered:
   Osmotic pressure. Relevant when membranes are implemented.
** Wright-Fisher Model                                         :discovered:
   Genetic drift in finite populations. Relevant when
   replication with variation is implemented.
** Michaelis-Menten Kinetics                                   :discovered:
   Enzyme catalysis saturation. Would deepen the catalysis model.
** Bond Equilibrium                                            :discovered:
   The current system has no bond-breaking in practice. Real chemistry
   reaches equilibrium where formation rate = breaking rate. This
   requires temperature feedback from exothermic bond heat release.
